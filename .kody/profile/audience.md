# Audience

A-Guy serves three groups, and most product decisions trace back to one of them:

**Students** — the primary users. They get learning that adapts to their pace
and gaps, instant contextual feedback from the tutor (explanations, not just
right/wrong), help scoped to exactly the exercise they're on, and progress that
persists across sessions. Rich math and diagrams are first-class, not bolted on.

**Educators & content creators** — they author and curate the material.
AI-assisted creation (import exercises from PDFs via Vision AI), a block-based
editor with rich text, multi-tenant isolation, draft/preview/version control,
and a full admin panel.

**Developers & platform builders** — they extend and operate the platform. A
modern, fully typed stack; an AI-ready architecture (vector search, embeddings,
memory built in); extensible collections, custom endpoints, and job queues;
Vercel-deployable.

The platform is Hebrew-first with English, aimed at the Israeli market, but
multi-locale and multi-tenant so it can serve more languages and organizations.
